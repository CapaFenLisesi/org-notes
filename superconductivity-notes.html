1.1 2.1 Landau Fermi-liquid <2014-02-09 日>

In a system of fermions, the ground states of the system has its Fermi momentum \(\mathbf{p}_F\), which is related to the total number of particles N in the system by:

\begin{equation} N=\sum_{k,s}=\sum_s \frac{V}{(2\pi)^3}\int d^3k=\sum_s frac{4\pi p_F ^3 V}{3 h^3} \end{equation}

This equation holds because pauli exclusion principle guarantees that N is just the number of states available in the system.
